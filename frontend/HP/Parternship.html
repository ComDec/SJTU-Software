Partnership

The two teams began to contact each other from the time of topic selection and brainstorming. During the preparation period, the communication between the two teams was not only about sharing resources, but also about having a clear understanding of each other's projects and making improvements to the core of the projects under the advice of the other team.

Finally, we are very grateful to IGEM for organizing this international event, which not only enables the two teams to get to know each other, but also enables the members of the two teams to gain such a rare friendship.

Partnership one :

SJTU-Software & Tongji_Software

Through China IGemer communication group, Yanping Zhao, leader of Tongji_Software, and Hu Yuanjie Hu, leader of SJTU-Software got in touch on April 20. Although the projects of two teams are not similar, because both of us act as software teams and have similar biological and computer backgrounds, we have made a lot of suggestions to each other in terms of project core and algorithms. In addition, we have helped each other in many ways.

Meeting between members of two teams

The leaders of the two teams successfully met offline for the first time on May 1

From April to May

During this period, the leaders of the two teams occasionally exchanged ideas via wechat about how the software teams could help the synthetic biologists and igemers remove obstacles.

It was worth mentioning that "We wanted to design a software to assist the design of Toehold RNA Switch" in the brainstorming of Tongji_Software Team at that time. Although Tongji_Software did not carry out this project, they accumulated a lot of knowledge during the investigation of this idea, including the prediction of secondary structure of RNA. This knowledge reserve facilitated the subsequent communication between the two teams.

In addition, the leaders of the two teams successfully met offline for the first time on May 1, thanks to the fact that both teams are based in Shanghai.

In June:

After the two teams roughly determined the project themes, we had a preliminary exchange with each other on respective project backgrounds, project significance, and methods involved. We mentioned the idea of building a DNA computing platform to assist in early cancer screening, mainly related to secondary structure prediction. Tongji_Software hopes to find a way to fight against super bacteria by comparing the sequence similarity between bacteriophages and bacteria. Both of us provide the helpful tools for the other team.

One-Hot encoding method

Two teams communicated online on July 4th.

From July to early Auguest

We proposed to Tongji_Software that "the prediction effect of RNA secondary structure is not good". After we introduced the whole project process, Tongji_Software found that we used the "one-hot" method when encoding nucleic acid sequence information. At this time, Yanping Zhao, leader of Tongji_Software, remembered that she had read a paper "Low-N Protein Engineering with Data-efficient deep Learning". This paper points out that "the sparse encoding obtained by using one-HOT encoding to represent the word vector has a large dimension and is isolated and unrelated between words, so semantic information between words cannot be expressed". After Tongji_Software told us this point of view, we carried out an investigation and changed the encoding method from "one-hot" to the current "word-to-vector".

During the offline communication between the two teams, Tongji_Software proposed that "the method currently used is sequence alignment——Spacer in bacterial genome is first found and then compared to phage genome. But this method has high accuracy and low detection rate." After communication, we put forward valuable suggestions to Tongji_Software. We thought that "since the detection rate of comparison method is too low, why not try non-comparison method?" It was at our suggestion that Tongji_Software added alight-free sections, which supplemented the project core and made the project more complete.

Two teams at CCIC conference

Since CCIC

At the CCIC meeting, the two teams communicated offline again. In this process, Tongji_Software pointed out that our project itself was too specialized and difficult to understand. After the discussion, we made improvements in project sharing and project presentation, which made it easier for the audience to understand.

In addition, the two teams communicated with each other about education, engineering and other sectors that had doubts, and the two teams had a clearer understanding of these sectors. We are also encouraged by Tongji_Software to set up popular science web pages and make popular science mini-games.During the process of developing the project and creating the wiki, the two teams will share some resources, such as front-end knowledge of the web, art vector material websites and etc.
